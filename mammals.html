Additional Info




Rank #1: Chinchilla

Chinchillas are smaller mammals, sitting at a length of 23-28cm. They have soft, silky fur and are very socialable. They are also very intelligent. However, they cannot survive in temps higher than 80 degrees Fahrenheit and are noctural creatures.

Rank #2: Marmoset Monkey

Marmoset monkeys are the very small, ranging in length from 12 to 16 cm. They live in the treetops and many live in hot rainforests. They also love to eat sap from trees and mostly give birth to twins each time. They are also considered the most primitive monkey and are used for research studies a lot.

Rank #3: Blue Whale

Blue whales are the largest species of whale and are found all over the planet. They follow seasonal migrations and are carnivores. Despite this fact, they really only eat krill. They are also known to be very loud.
